Biography

U2 is an Irish rock band formed in Dublin in 1976. The group consists of Bono (lead vocals and rhythm guitar), The Edge (lead guitar, keyboards, and backing vocals), Adam Clayton (bass guitar), and Larry Mullen Jr. (drums and percussion). Known for their expansive sound and political themes, U2 became one of the most successful bands in the world, blending post-punk roots with spiritual and social consciousness.

Band Members

  • Bono (Paul David Hewson) – Lead vocals and rhythm guitar.
  • The Edge (David Howell Evans) – Lead guitar, keyboards, and backing vocals.
  • Adam Clayton – Bass guitar.
  • Larry Mullen Jr. – Drums and percussion.

Each member has remained with the band since its founding, a rare continuity in the music industry.

Discography Highlights

  • War (1983) – Featured the hit “Sunday Bloody Sunday”.
  • The Joshua Tree (1987) – Their landmark album with “With or Without You” and “Where the Streets Have No Name”.
  • Achtung Baby (1991) – Marked a creative reinvention with a darker, more experimental sound.
  • All That You Can't Leave Behind (2000) – Included the inspirational “Beautiful Day”.
  • Songs of Experience (2017) – A reflective work balancing maturity and energy.

Awards & Recognition

  • 22 Grammy Awards – more than any other band in history.
  • Inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2005.
  • Over 170 million records sold worldwide.
  • Multiple humanitarian awards for global activism and charity work.

Bono’s activism and U2’s dedication to social justice have made them cultural icons beyond music.

Fun Facts

  • Before U2, they were briefly called “Feedback” and “The Hype”.
  • The Edge’s signature sound comes from heavy use of delay effects.
  • U2 once performed on a rooftop in Los Angeles for a music video — echoing The Beatles’ famous final concert.
  • Their “360° Tour” (2009–2011) became the highest-grossing concert tour of all time.
  • Bono has been nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize multiple times.
